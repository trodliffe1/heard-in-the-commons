Local Bus Services

Labour MPs, including Sam Rushworth, Michael Wheeler, and Jade Botterill, are raising concerns about the government's commitment to improving local bus services, which are essential for community connectivity. They are seeking clarity on the government's plans to tackle existing challenges and emphasize the need for reliable public transport. In response, the government has introduced the Bus Services (No. 2) Bill, which empowers local leaders to enhance services and has allocated over £1 billion for these improvements. MPs highlighted the impact of transport costs on children and young people's access to education and leisure, urging the Minister to prioritize local needs in the new bus system.

Residents in Worsley and Eccles are particularly affected by severe road congestion, prompting local representatives to call for integrated transport networks to alleviate traffic issues. The government is addressing this through the "English Devolution" White Paper, which aims to improve coordination among strategic authorities. Local advocates, including Jade Botterill, are demanding accountability from bus companies, while Simon Lightwood supports transport reforms.

The new bus services Bill will require local authorities to identify and protect essential services, particularly in rural areas. Concerns remain about the dominance of private operators and the lack of minimum service requirements. Additionally, discussions have highlighted challenges for disabled individuals regarding bus pass usage restrictions. The government has invested £700 million in the concessionary travel scheme and is revising funding formulas to enhance local flexibility, allowing communities to tailor transport programs to their specific needs.
