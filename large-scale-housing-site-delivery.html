Large Scale Housing Site Delivery

Matthew Pennycook, the Minister for Housing and Planning, has underscored the importance of large-scale housing developments in England, aiming to build 1.5 million homes during this Parliament to stimulate economic growth. The government is reviving the concept of new towns, drawing inspiration from successful post-war initiatives that provided affordable housing. In September 2024, an independent New Towns Taskforce was established to explore potential sites for new towns, receiving over 100 proposals for communities of at least 10,000 residents. The taskforce aims to create sustainable, well-designed towns with essential infrastructure, including public transport, healthcare, and education.

To support these efforts, the government has identified 350 sites capable of accommodating around 700,000 homes, focusing on key locations such as Frome Gateway in Bristol and Beam Park in London. A £3 million grant will assist local authorities, alongside additional funding for statutory consultees and the Building Safety Regulator. The New Homes Accelerator, launched in July 2024, seeks to expedite housing delivery on large sites, potentially providing over 28,500 homes.

Furthermore, the government is investing £29.6 million from the brownfield infrastructure fund to develop 1,000 homes in Broadford City Village and an additional £1.5 million for a new district in Manchester. A £20 million allocation for the Brownfield Land Release Fund aims to remediate underutilized land, addressing housing shortages while promoting environmental sustainability. The taskforce will report to the Deputy Prime Minister and Prime Minister by summer, outlining strategies for funding and development.
