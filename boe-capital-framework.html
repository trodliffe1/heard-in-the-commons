Bank of England Capital Framework Review

Emma Reynolds, the Economic Secretary to the Treasury, announced the completion of a five-year review of the Bank of England's capital framework, originally established in 2018. The review concluded that the framework successfully meets its objectives for monetary and financial stability, with current capital parameters considered adequate for the Bank's balance sheet. The existing financial arrangements with HM Treasury are deemed sufficient for the Bank's transition to a demand-driven operating framework supported by repurchase agreements (repos).

The updated Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) between the Bank and HM Treasury will be continuously reviewed to ensure ongoing engagement during this transition. The MoU specifies capital framework parameters, including a floor of £0.5 billion, a target of £3.5 billion, and a ceiling of £5.5 billion, reflecting a structured approach to capital management.

In a recent lecture at the London School of Economics, Victoria Saporta emphasized the importance of readiness for repos in the current economic climate, highlighting their role in providing liquidity and stability to financial markets. She addressed potential challenges posed by market volatility and underscored the necessity for robust frameworks to manage these risks. Saporta advocated for proactive measures, including enhanced communication and collaboration among financial institutions, to effectively navigate future economic uncertainties.

The Bank of England's transition to a repo-led operating framework aims to improve the efficiency of monetary policy, with key resources including a letter from the Governor and a detailed transition paper.
