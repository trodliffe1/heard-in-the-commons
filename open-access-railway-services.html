Open Access Railway Services

Transport Secretary Heidi Alexander has underscored the vital role of open access operators in the reformed Great British railways, backing proposals from Wrexham Shropshire and Midlands Railway while ensuring existing track access rights remain intact. She stressed that open access should improve passenger services without sacrificing quality or taxpayer value. Concerns were raised by Transport Secretary Wendy Morton regarding the inclusion of Aldridge train station in the proposed Wrexham to London Euston line. Additionally, issues surrounding inadequate rail services in Easington were highlighted, with calls for Grand Central to be permitted to stop at local stations. Alexander advocated for open access operators, which currently account for just 1% of the railways, promoting a balanced approach to enhance rail network capacity and service options.

In related developments, Peter Swallow discussed the new Go-op mandate for train services between Swindon and Taunton, reflecting the Government's commitment to improving connectivity and exploring similar enhancements for Berkshire. Alexander expressed her support for this initiative, which aims to establish the UK's first co-operatively owned train service, fostering community involvement in transportation. Meanwhile, Mark Francois voiced frustration over Greater Anglia's failure to rebuild Wickford station, citing a pattern of unfulfilled promises. A senior official is considering escalating the matter to the Secretary of State, while Alexander has offered to facilitate a meeting with the Rail Minister to expedite the project.
