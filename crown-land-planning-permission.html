Crown Land - Planning Permission for Development

Housing and Planning Minister Matthew Pennycook has announced significant reforms to the planning processes for Crown land, emphasizing the need for improvements in light of challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic. The existing urgent Crown development route, introduced in 2006, has never been utilized. To rectify this, the Government is implementing two new planning permission routes under the Levelling-up and Regeneration Act 2023. The first route allows applications deemed of "national importance" to bypass local authorities and go directly to the Planning Inspectorate, aiming to streamline decision-making while maintaining local engagement. The second route updates the urgent Crown development process, enabling the Secretary of State to rapidly determine urgent applications.

Pennycook stressed the importance of cross-party support for these changes, advocating for transparency and safeguards in the use of powers related to national development decisions. The Secretary of State for Housing, Communities and Local Government will assess projects classified as nationally important, which may include those affecting national security or public services. A three-step transparency process will notify relevant Members of Parliament and local authorities at key stages of the application process, with an annual report detailing decisions made.

New draft regulations will amend the Town and Country Planning Act 1990, establishing the two Crown development routes, which are subject to parliamentary debate and approval by April 2025. The Government aims to enhance efficiency and accountability in planning for significant developments while ensuring that stakeholders are adequately informed throughout the process.
