National Procurement Policy

The UK Government has unveiled a national procurement policy statement aimed at optimizing the £400 billion spent annually on public procurement, effective February 24, 2023. This initiative, aligned with the Procurement Act 2023, prioritizes economic growth, support for small businesses, innovation, and job creation. Key measures include streamlining procurement processes for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) and voluntary community and social enterprises (VCSEs). Starting April 1, 2025, government departments will be required to set three-year spending targets for SMEs, with VCSEs following in 2026. Regular spot checks will ensure timely payments to smaller suppliers.

The policy emphasizes maximizing procurement spending with SMEs and VCSEs, enhancing collaboration, and reserving low-value contracts for local businesses. A new digital commercial centre of excellence will be established to improve procurement processes and assist SMEs in their digital transformation. This centre will focus on enhancing commercial capabilities, creating an online register for agreements to boost transparency, and utilizing artificial intelligence to streamline operations.

Additionally, a consultation will be conducted to reform public procurement, with a proposed public interest test to help contracting authorities decide between outsourcing and in-house work. This strategy aims to ensure procurement decisions align with taxpayer value and government priorities, ultimately maximizing public procurement's potential to create jobs and strengthen supply chains, benefiting the economy and communities nationwide.
