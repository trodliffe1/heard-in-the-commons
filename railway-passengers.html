Rail Passengers

The UK Government, under the leadership of Secretary of State for Transport Heidi Alexander, is making strides to enhance rail performance through the establishment of Great British Railways. This initiative aims to streamline track and train management, ultimately benefiting passengers and taxpayers alike. A cornerstone of this effort is the TransPennine route upgrade, the largest rail infrastructure project in the UK, which promises to double seating capacity between Huddersfield and Manchester and increase it by 30% between Huddersfield and Leeds. This upgrade is expected to improve service reliability and stimulate local economic growth by prioritizing local employment—60% of the workforce will be recruited from within 25 miles of the route, and 66% of spending will go to local suppliers.

In a related development, MP John Lamont is advocating for the extension of the Borders Railway from Tweedbank to Hawick and Newcastleton, emphasizing its potential for job creation and economic growth. He has pressed the UK Government for funding for a feasibility study linked to this project, part of the Borderlands growth deal. Alexander has indicated that discussions with Scottish officials are ongoing, with further information anticipated in the upcoming spending review. The Prime Minister has also promised to share details of this review soon, which could significantly impact the government's fiscal strategies and priorities moving forward. Regular updates on the TransPennine project and its local impact are expected from Alexander.
